2022-2020

Title: Project Officer

Team: Collection (Huis van Alijn) / Data Intervention Team (Collections of Ghent)

Description:

At Het Huis van Alijn I was employed as a project officer for the project Collections of Ghent (CoGhent)*. My main tasks for this project included improving the data quality of the collection before publication, providing data, images and content for stories in the CoGent box, social media and cocreation projects. Lastly, I wrote documentation for developers who wanted to use the data architecture set up during the project and organized workshops for working with this architecture, specifically the SparQL endpoint. At het Huis van Alijn I helped with the registration of certain collections. Lastly I was in charge of the visualisation and quality management of collection data. This included developing certain tools for data visualisation and quality management.

* Collections of Ghent was a 3-year project funded by the Urban Innovative Actions (UIA) initiative of the European Regional Development Fund (ERDF). CoGhent brought together the collections of 4 museums (Huis van Alijn, STAM, Industriemuseum, Design Museum Gent) and one archive (Archief Gent) in the city of Ghent, Belgium. It aimed to publish 100.000 heritage objects on an online platform. In addition, this heritage was brought closer to the citizens of Ghent through the CoGent box, a travelling digital experience room, where visitors could see heritage object come to live through stories, connect to their past, and share and experience this with other citizens of Ghent. Lastly I was also possible for citizens to add their own memories and images to the collection, enriching the existing collections. In doing so the project aimed to improve social cohesion in Ghent.
